THE PERFECT GAME

First Time

There’s a lot to say about Breath of the Wild. I first played it when it released in the spring of 2017. My cousin brought his switch to school and let me play it during my free period of my senior year. Just a few months away from graduating high school. That opening scene is something else. I was hyped to jump into this world.

The Hunt

It would take a few months for me to finally get my own switch, due to how quickly drops would sell out. It was summer when I finally got one. I had shown up early to Toys R Us (remember that?) and waited. I was 11th in line. “Surely I would get one”, I thought. After one to two hours of waiting, the worker comes out and announces they have 10 consoles. Heartbreak. I quickly opened my wallet and offered the guy in front me the $100 bill that I had planned to use on games for his ticket. He refused. Instead he handed me the ticket and wished me happy gaming. He said he already has one and was just going to get one for his coworker's kid. Guess he figured some random 18 year old was a good enough substitute. I secured a console.

Copy Secured

I loved my switch and would fight with my sibling over who got to play. BOTW blew me away and I poured close to 100 hours on it. I never beat Ganon, however. I didn’t want the game to end and eventually, I played other games. So there it sat for years, until TOTK released and I told myself I would finally beat BOTW to prepare. I did not. I don’t even know if I played much further than finishing The Great Plateau. And there it sat again, collecting dust in my backlog. “How can I, the biggest Zelda fan, not have beat BOTW?”, I would think. Years passed again.

Now the year is 2026 and I am a fresh uni grad whose sibling gifted him a switch 2. And BOTW has a switch 2 upgrade (oooo 60fps!). So I bought the upgrade and months passed. Then sometime in February or March, I started playing BOTW again. This time in higher resolution and 60fps. Beautiful. I’m enjoying every minute and exploring every nook and crack. The world is so expertly designed to always give you 5 different things you want to see.

I hit 10 hours, then 20, then 40. I think it’s time I beat Ganon. I start prepping by wrapping up a few side quests first and getting more hearts and stamina. About 50 hours in now, okay I’m ready. I ascend Hyrule Castle and fight Ganon. Such a cool fight, though a bit easy. At least, Zelda’s appearance and support in the fight was really cool.

So there it happened. 9 years later, I finally beat BOTW. Wow. I have a lot to say but it can really be summed up in a few words: It’s the perfect game.

Music

This is the most quiet Zelda game ever. You go into the field, climb a mount, go gliding, go riding, go sliding. Whatever it might be, the game seldom plays music in the open world when you’re exploring. You hear Link’s footsteps and his clothes and weapons moving around. You hear animals, like birds chirping and fish jumping. You hear a slow, faint, lonely piano.

Some video I saw said it best: It’s painfully clear that it’s only you, a hero who lost the fight, lost his friends, and lost his memories.

Freedom

This is the first Zelda since the first that doesn’t hold your hand through everything. Aside from the tutorial area, you are free to go where you want and complete (or skip) the dungeons and bosses in any order you want. Shrines and Koroks are the same way. The world is hostile and it will be clear when you need to turn around, but if you have the motivation and skill, you can push through. This amount of freedom is refreshing even 9 years later after release. It’s the easier, safer route to just funnel the character down a specific path and order. Not Zelda though.

Final Thoughts

I truly love this game. It is the definition of a 10/10. The world is a fully lived in, fully realized setting with history and purpose. It’s so diverse too. You can have the four corners: Desert, Lake, Snow, Volcano. You also have jungles, mountains, canyons, rivers, oceans, swamps, etc etc etc. Each with their own challenges and secrets, waiting for you to find them, There are towns, both new and recurring, with beautiful music to accompany them. The one I always think of first is Tarrey Town, with more instruments being added with each resident you bring in. Incredible. To avoid rambling, I’ll wrap up by saying: BOTW is an epic game with an epic scale, combined with game designers and artists who clearly have so much passion and drive. Breath of the Wild is my favorite game.
