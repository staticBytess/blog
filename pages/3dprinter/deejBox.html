Deej Volume Mixer Box


The Problem

When I game, I usually have music and discord open too. The issue is when I need to lower or raise the volume of a single channel. If I'm mid-game, I need to tab out then find the volume slider for that single app. Additonally, volume levels are rarely consistent. A song might be louder than the one before, or the game might have a loud section for a few minutes. It gets annoying fast having to tab out and change the volume levels between 3 seperate apps.

The Idea

I bought an audio mixer (shown above, with the blue sticky notes) that takes in 3 input streams and sends them out through a single output, with a dials for each channel to lower or raise the volume. I LOVE it. Since I use a monitor without built in speakers and play consoles, I used to switch switch the aux input between my monitor and PC. Not the worst thing but it was annoying and it meant I couln't game on my consoles while talking on discord. The audio mixer fixed this.
I wanted this exact set up but for seperate apps instead of seperate aux inputs. Specifically, I want 3 knobs: One for the current game, one for discord, and the third for my music.

The Solution

While researching, I found a solution called Deej that promised to do exactly that. You provide a microcontroller with some code on it, and Deej reads that data and handles converting it to volume settings.

I orded these HiLetgo 10K ohm potentiometers off of amazon and tried to solder them onto my raspberry pico. Turns out I'm trash at soldering so I asked my friend to help me. I sent him the schematic and which pins I needed soldered to the data (ADC 0, ADC1, ADC2). He returned it to me and I plugged in it, excited to test it out. Well, it didn't turn on. I forgot to specify I needed the 3v3 OUT pin and not 3v3_EN. So I return it to him and he fixes it. Problem solved! I had printed a case and I assemble everything. In the process, I must have twisted or yanked too hard because one knob was fluctating like crazy, and another's data cable had come loose. Great. Send it back, get it returned, and test again. This time all knobs are flucating like crazy. Fantastic.
For my sanity, I asked him to solder new potentionmeters here where I could immediately test it. This worked out great and with my new case, I was able to assemble it and it works great now.
